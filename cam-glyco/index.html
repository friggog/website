WHAT WE DO

Overconsumption of sugar is one of the world's greatest public health crises, causing conditions such as heart disease, obesity and diabetes. The most impactful way to solve this problem is through the reformulation of food and beverages to reduce their sugar content. Cambridge Glycoscience is developing ingredients that replace sugar in food products, without compromising on price and functionality. Our products are…

DELICIOUS

Our ingredients allow 1:1 sugar replacement in foods, such as bakery products and snacks. They do this by replicating the various roles that sugar serves in food besides sweetness, such as texture, browning and caramelisation - with no aftertaste.

HEALTHY

Less is more. Our ingredients are low-calorie, low-sugar and low-Glycaemic Index, making them perfect for a healthy diet. They are also high in fiber, which is great for boosting gut microbial health.

AFFORDABLE

Most sweeteners are more expensive than sugar on a per-weight basis. We aim to change this by making our sugar substitutes for same price as sugar, enabling low-sugar food products to become a mainstream reality.

NATURAL

Our ingredients are plant-based, non-GMO products. By using an abundant agricultural co-product for our extraction process, we also ensure that our ingredients are sustainably sourced.

 
 
 

WHO WE ARE

TOM SIMMONS, PhD

Co-Founder & CEO

Tom gained over 10 years of experience in glycoscience through his work at the University of Cambridge and the University of Edinburgh, bringing a deep scientific understanding to his management of the company. During this time, he also served as CSO at SugarWise and held a position on the World Economic Forum's Global Futures Council.

RUBEN TADMOR, MSci

Co-Founder & COO

Ruben drives the scale-up and commercialisation of Cambridge Glycoscience's proprietary technology platform. He holds a Master's degree in Natural Sciences from the University of Cambridge and has experience in commercialising intellectual property. He also worked on developing the marketing strategy at a previous SME.

PROF. PAUL DUPREE, PhD

Co-Founder & Scientific Advisor

Paul is a Professor of Biochemistry at the University of Cambridge. His research group hosted the BBSRC's Sustainable Bioenergy Centre Cell Wall Sugars Programme, developed strategies to improve plants and enzymes for increased sugar release from biomass. His lab has made seminal discoveries in carbohydrate biosynthesis, structure and enzymology.
