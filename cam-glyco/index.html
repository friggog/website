Cambridge Glycoscience is making sugar substitutes that work in food - so you can have your cake and eat it too!

WHAT WE DO

Cambridge Glycoscience develops functional food ingredients for a low-sugar, high-fibre, all-around healthy food system. Overconsumption of sugar is one of the world's greatest public health crises - causing heart disease, obesity, diabetes and more early deaths in the US than any other factor. The most impactful way of resolving the problem is through product reformulation of food and beverages.

WHO WE ARE

TOM SIMMONS, PhD

Co-Founder & CEO

Tom has led Cambridge Glycoscience since inception. With over 8 years experience in the glycoscience, particularly in carbohydrate chemistry, enzymology and NMR, Tom brings a deep scientific expertise to his management of the company.

Tom has appeared as a scientific expert on Sky News and Channel 4 television, among others. He sits on the World Economic Forum's GFC Council for the future of innovation & entrepreneurship and blogs regularly on the topic. Tom holds a PhD in biochemistry from the University of Edinburgh and a Diploma in entrepreneurship from Judge Business School, Cambridge.

RUBEN TADMOR, MSci

Co-Founder & COO

Ruben drives the scale-up and commercialisation of Cambridge Glycoscience's proprietary technology platform. He also guides the business strategy of the company.

Ruben holds a first-class Master's degree in Biochemistry and Systems Biology from the University of Cambridge. He has a background in tech transfer through the commercialisation of two patented university technologies. He also worked on developing the marketing strategy at a previous SME.

PROF. PAUL DUPREE, PhD

Co-Founder & Scientific Advisor

Paul provides the scientific and technical expertise on which our products and services are based, with twenty years experience in glycoscience research. He is responsible for guiding Cambridge Glycoscience's broader scientific vision.

Paul is a Professor of Plant Biochemistry at the University of Cambridge. His lab hosted the BBSRC's Sustainable Bioenergy Centre Cell Wall Sugars Programme, which developed strategies to improve plants and enzymes for increased sugar release from biomass. In this time, his lab has made seminal discoveries in carbohydrate biosynthesis, structure and enzymology.
